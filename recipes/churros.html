  • Churros
  • Churros are a delicious fried dough pastry with a combination of crispy exterior and fluffy interior. Originating from Spain, they have become popular in many cultures. Making them involves a simple process of piping pastry dough through a star-shaped nozzle directly into hot oil.

Prep Time: ~20-30 mins | Servings: 12-20 churros

Directions

  1. Combine sugar and cinnamon in a shallow bowl, set aside
  2. Mix flour, baking powder and salt in a bowl.
  3. Add oil and water and mix until just combined, like a wet sticky dough
  4. Transfer dough into a piping bag with a star tip nozzle, or else they'll look like hot dogs
  5. Heat 2 inches of oil over medium high in a pot to 340F
  6. Pipe 6 inches of dough into the oil and cut with scissors, make sure to not over crowd
  7. Cook for 2-3 minutes or until golden brown
  8. Remove onto paper towel to drain, then roll in sugar while still hot
  9. For the chocolate sauce, place chocolate in microwave for 30 second increments and mix in small amounts of heavy cream until smooth

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cup refined sugar
  • 2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 cup all purpose flour
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 tbsp vegetable, canola, or olive oil (not extra virgin olive oil)
  • 1 cup boiling water
  • 2 cups vegetable or canola oil , for frying
  • 1/2 cup dark chocolate or semi sweet chocolate chips
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • Piping bag and star tip
